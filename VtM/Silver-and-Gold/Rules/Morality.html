Have you thought about the moral costs of blood bonds and ghouls? No? Well, it's that time!

What happens when you fully blood bond someone to you? Well, there's a great examination of how the rules have interpreted this more than once that I stole from here and added to our wiki as well. Basically, the first thing to know is that, although the regnant is the most important person in the thrall's life, that doesn't mean that they are exactly slave. Blood Bond ≠ Obedient Servant, at least, not necessarily. Partly it depends on your Thrall's Nature, and partly on their Willpower. Nature archetypes can react very differently to being bonded. Consider three bonded individuals, one who is a Child, Competitor, and a third who is a Survivor. Imagine how these three beings might treat the object of their obsession. What might be different if they were treated well, or treated poorly by their Regnant? What if their Regnant gave them an order? Now, to make things interesting, imagine how these same three beings might treat each other, in respect to their relationships with the Regnant? Jealousy is fun!

Now, consider separately, Ghouls. You do not​ have to blood bond someone to make them a ghoul. You simply give them (at least) a point / pint of your blood to imbibe. Congratulations, you've just addicted them to a drug more insidious than any other. Yes, they are addicted. After one month of having no vitae in their system, Ghouls go through withdrawal for (6-Stamina) weeks. Oh, and they age, too; since vampire vitae halts aging, having none in one's system can be devastating. What does the withdrawal do to them? Well, any time they have a chance to gain vitae, they roll against Self-Control (Dif 7) or attempt to get it however possible. And, while I make no judgments of my own here, addicts aren't known for exercising restraint in pursuit of a fix.

A Ghoul that is blood bonded to you? Well, now you have an obsessed addict to play with. This relationship can very much be the equivalent of slavery. 

Okay, so you know all this. What's the potential Humanity cost for making a ghoul? Well, this depends on the specific scenario. At the very least, it's a Humanity 8 sin of Injury to another (which means, for most of you, not a problem). There's a pretty good discussion about it here, where levels of 8, 7, 3, and even 1 are proposed. However, in our campaign, it will most often be a Humanity 5 sin. Why? Looking at the description for Humanity levels 5-6 (per the RAW): 

People die. Stuff breaks. A vampire below the cultural human norm has little difficulty with the fact that she needs blood to survive, and she does what needs to be done to get it. Though she won’t necessarily go out of her way to destroy property or end a victim’s life, she accepts that sometimes that’s what fate has in store for some folks.

​This will be modified by the circumstances. Your long-time ally, who knows your secret (you Masquerade-breaker you!), is badly wounded. You have time to tell her that you can give her blood that will save her life, but it comes at a great cost. She gives informed consent: Humanity 8 sin. You need some fodder to delay the Methuselah Tzimisce while you retreat, so you dominate an innocent passerby and force them to drink your blood. You know they have no hope of surviving, and your intent isn't to give them a fighting chance, but simply to gain a few extra seconds while they die horribly. Humanity 3 sin, possibly lower.

What about blood bonding? Blood bonding in and of itself (generally) requires no Humanity roll; exceptions are possible for certain acts. What you do with (and to) the Thrall might. This is the same as any other type of coercive means; if you Dominate someone and force them kill their loved one for your pleasure, you would roll for Humanity loss. 

As to why you don't blood bond your childe entirely, beyond social etiquette? Well, because people who are fully bonded aren't sane, they're not easy to be around and not even very useful. They're not just madly in love with you, they're obsessed, they're enthralled, they'll do strange and weird and dangerous stuff to get your attention, your love, any interaction with you. Imagine not just love but a quite debilitating way to have a relationship all the way to a mental issue.

"What IS this?" "It's the head of the Harpy who said your shirt was ugly, father! Now she's gone! She's gone forever!"

Or: "Childe, my car is aflame. Who could have done this?" "It was me! I am so sorry! But you IGNORED me!"
 

Most beings that are blood bonded are quite jealous of their relationship with their Regnant, although that jealously may play out in very different ways. One Thrall may crave the feeling of power they get from the Regnant, and another may feel a bond of what seems to be true love. If you've seen the movie "The Favourite," you get a picture of what it's like to have two Thrall's in competition for the attentions of their Regnant. However, with mortal (non-ghouls) who are blood bonded, they aren't likely to do anything too crazy, although that is dependent on the specific personality archetype and character history. Ghouls, though? That's where things can get messy. 

a quick example I can think of that have happened in a game:

a long time Brujah ghoul (300 years or so) went on a jealous rampage during the day one time and killed off all of his Domitor's other ghouls. The player woke up to his haven covered in blood, with his oldest ghoul exhausted and injured from the fight. All because he (the vampire) had sent one of his newer ghouls on an assignment that the oldest one thought he should have gone on.

The blood bond can be a really scary thing.
 

Finally, remember the Masquerade. You are responsible for what your Ghouls do, especially if the breach the Masquerade. One thing you should note (for me!) is how much your ghouls know or reasonably could figure out. How do you feed them? What do they know of your nature and theirs? Etc. 

​TL;DR, the real disadvantage to ghouls and blood bonds is not the maintenance cost, but what these obsessed addicts and lovestruck fools might do.

PS - Oh wait, you might say. What about Humanity costs and Masquerade breaches for vampires on a different path than Humanity and don't abide by the Camarilla's rules? Neither of those things matters to them. Tzimisce were known for making armies of ghouls and effectively welding them together to create humungous creatures to devastate cities. Good luck!
